Redesigning Chess Opening Preparation

Making opening revision easier for chess players.

PROBLEM

“The seperation of information source and visual representation in chess opening preparation leads to inefficiency and time wastage. Existing attempts at combining the two, by the use of digital applications are unintuitive, bloated and not mobile friendly.”

In order to study chess openings, one needs to have 2 things:

  • Information source: Books, chess engines, videos
  • Visual Representation: Chess board, physical or digital

    • Earlier, the most obvious choices were a book and a physical board. This has many problems:

  • One has to constantly alternate attention between the book and the board.
  • It takes a lot of steps to study a particular part of your opening repertoire, e.g.:
  • Find a space
  • Set the board to the interested position
  • Getting to a particular page of the book
  • Chess openings can’t be studied while travelling because you need a space to set the board and the book.
