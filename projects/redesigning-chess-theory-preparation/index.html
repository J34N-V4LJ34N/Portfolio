Redesigning Chess Theory Preparation

Making chess theory revision easier for chess players.

PROBLEM

“The seperation of information source and visual representation in chess theory preparation leads to inefficiency and time wastage. Existing attempts at combining the two, by the use of digital applications are unintuitive, bloated and not mobile friendly.”

In order to study chess theory, one needs to have 2 things:

  • Information source: Books, chess engines, videos
  • Visual Representation: Chess board, physical or digital

    • Earlier, the most obvious choices were a book and a physical board. This has many problems:

  • One has to constantly alternate attention between the book and the board.
  • It takes a lot of steps to study a particular part of your theory repertoire, e.g.:
  • Find a space
  • Set the board to the interested position
  • Getting to a particular page of the book
  • Chess theory can’t be studied while travelling because you need a space to set the board and the book.

    About

    George is a college student who plays chess. He was born in USA and taught chess by his grandfather. Loses drawn endgames and draws winning endagames too often.

    Aim

  • Get better at playing chess at larger time-controls.
  • Improve classical USCF rating by 200 points in one year.
  • Spend the time in between classes to learn endgames.

    • Pain Points

  • No space or time to use a chessboard in a classroom.
  • Finds it hard to study chess endgmes with only a book

    • Ravi

    Retired

    About

    Ravi is a person who enjoys his retired life playing casual online chess. He find chess openings fascinating. But when he tries to play the openings he likes he forgets the move order.

    Aim

  • Learn about chess openings and the grandmasters who play them
  • Memorise his favourite openings

    • Pain Points

  • Finds it hard to find time to concentrate on learning chess openings.
  • When he does find time he is overwhelmed at the number of moves. Not able to schedule learning a managable set of moves reguraly.
